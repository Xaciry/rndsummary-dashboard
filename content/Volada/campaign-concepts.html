Campaign 1

"You've Been Drinking the Wrong Thing"

Brand Indictment Film — 75-second hero + 5 platform-native cuts
Problem Being Solved

Volada is spending shelf space and distributor relationships competing with Deep Eddy — which has national distribution, Heaven Hill's backing, and documented TV advertising — while having no video presence at all. A single third-party TikTok creator appears to be generating more brand visibility than the company's own 179 Instagram posts.

Primary Outcome

Brand recall at Total Wine; YouTube pre-roll against competitor search terms

If Not Executed

Deep Eddy continues owning the shelf conversation. Volada's first-mover claim goes unannounced.

Campaign 2

"Three Flavors. No Rules."

SKU-First Cocktail Series — 3 recipe videos + flavor comparison + paid variations
Problem Being Solved

Volada has three visually distinct, culturally specific SKUs and zero video showing what they taste like or how to use them. Recipe-format content on TikTok is the lowest-friction path to follower growth for a spirits brand at this stage, and Volada doesn't have a single piece of it.

Primary Outcome

Launch Volada TikTok channel; 10,000+ views in first 30 days; retail partner repost assets

If Not Executed

Brand continues to be represented on TikTok only by third-party UGCthe brand has no control over.

Campaign 2

"Three Flavors. No Rules."

SKU-first cocktail series — 3 recipe videos + flavor comparison + paid variations · No additional shoot day required
Core Idea
Three videos, one per SKU. Each opens with the bottle in frame — no context, no setup — and immediately demonstrates a cocktail build. The VO (or on-screen text) is four words or fewer per beat: "Real watermelon. Real agave. Done." The cuts happen at the moment of pour, the ice drop, and the first sip. No slow dissolves. No acoustic guitar. Treat a fruit vodka like it's dangerous, because compared to what else is on the shelf, it is.

Why No Second Shoot Day Is Needed
Production Consolidation Logic
Campaign 2 exists because Campaign 1's production system has spare capacity in Blocks 2 and 3. The two-hour product beauty setup (Block 2) that captures Campaign 1's pour sequences produces more raw footage than Campaign 1 needs. Rather than leaving that time unused, Block 2 is structured to also deliver all individual SKU pours for the recipe videos, the flavor comparison side-by-side, and ingredient close-up cutaways.
Block 3 (social bar setup) was originally scoped for Campaign 1's lifestyle B-roll. It's extended by 30–45 minutes to capture the three full cocktail builds for the SKU recipe videos. Same lighting setup, same dressed bar, same crew. Campaign 2 adds 9 deliverables to the day at the cost of roughly 45 minutes of incremental production time. The marginal cost per additional deliverable approaches zero.

Production Plan

Single Shoot Day — 3 Setup Blocks

Consolidated shoot day structure · 5-person crew · No second day required for either campaign
Day Structure
1
7:00 AM – 10:30 AM · 2.5 hrs incl. setup
Block 1 — Founder Interview & Direct-Address
·Single-source key light (large softbox camera left), negative fill (black V-flat camera right), textured background (brick, adobe, or warm painted wall)
·Ben on-camera: all hero VO lines, all direct-address cuts, B-roll of Ben handling product
·Captures: Campaign 1 hero film segments, Cuts 1, 3, 5 founder moments, scripted safety takes
·Reset: 30 min — clear furniture, dress product table, adjust light to overhead beauty setup
2
11:00 AM – 1:00 PM · 2 hrs incl. setup · Shared Block
Block 2 — Product Beauty & Pour Shots
·Overhead or 45-degree product beauty setup. LED panel or HMI bounced into white card. Black or tiled surface. All three SKUs dressed.
·Campaign 1: all product close-up footage, pour sequences for Cuts 2 and 4, condensation and texture shots
·Campaign 2: all individual SKU pours for recipe videos, flavor comparison side-by-side, ingredient close-up cutaways
·Slow-motion on all pours (120fps minimum) — dedicated sub-list within block; at least 3 selectable pours per SKU per angle
·Reset: 30 min — add glassware, garnishes, dress bar surface for social setup
3
1:30 PM – 4:00 PM · 2 hrs + 30–45 min extension · Shared Block
Block 3 — Cocktail Serve & Lifestyle
·Social bar setup: backlit bottles in background, garnished drinks in foreground, talent (Ben or supporting talent) in frame
·Campaign 1: lifestyle B-roll, Ben picking up finished glass for edit continuity, garnish/ice detail shots
·Campaign 2: three full cocktail build sequences (Watermelon, Hibiscus, Mango Chamoy), serve shots, sip and reaction beats
·TikTok-native framing: 9:16 vertical for all primary captures — DP shoots 9:16 first, then reframes for 16:9 on hero angles
